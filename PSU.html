Power Supply Unit (PSU)

Power Supply Unit (also known as PSU) converts alternating high voltage current (AC) to direct current (DC). It regulates the DC output voltage to the required power needed for the computer and its components.


Types of PSU:

  • + Non-Modular Power Supply - where all power supply cables are attached permanently
  • + Semi-Modular Power Supply - where some important power supply cables are attached
  • + Fully Modular Power Supply - where there are no power supply cables permanently attached


Different Sizes of PSU:

  • + PS/2 - Personal System/2
  • + ATX - Advanced Technology EXtended *
  • + mATX - Micro Advanced Technology EXtended *
  • + FlexATX - Flex Advanced Technology EXtended
  • + EPS - Entry-Level Power Supply
  • + SFX - Small Form Factor EXtended*
  • + TFX - Thin Form Factor EXtended
  • + WTX - Workstation Technology EXtended

* Most used PSU Sizes on modern computer systems.
